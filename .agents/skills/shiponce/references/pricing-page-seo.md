# Pricing Page SEO and Conversion

Use this when building or reviewing an outbound product site's Price/Pricing page. Treat the page as a conversion page with SEO intent, not as a plain price list.

## Core Principle

The Pricing page must make the price feel justified before asking the user to choose. First build value, then show the price, then remove doubts and route each buyer to the right plan.

## Page Structure

1. State the product value near the top: what pain it solves, who it is for, and what outcome the buyer gets.
2. Show price cards after the value frame, not as disconnected numbers.
3. Include a clear price anchor:
   - cost of manual work, agency work, missed leads, engineering time, or competitor alternatives
   - savings in time, money, effort, risk, or operational overhead
4. Offer plan choices for different buyers:
   - Starter or Free for low-friction entry
   - Pro or Growth as the recommended mainstream plan
   - Business or Enterprise for scale, security, support, or custom usage
   - Custom pricing when usage, seats, API calls, or service scope vary widely
5. Add a comparison table after the cards when feature differences matter.
6. Add trust and objection-handling blocks:
   - cancellation/refund/trial policy
   - usage limits, seats, credits, storage, API calls, and overage rules
   - support level, security/privacy claims, payment safety, and customer proof
7. Finish with FAQ and a decisive CTA.

## Copywriting Rules

- Do not lead with "Basic $19 / Pro $49" unless the product is already self-evident.
- Turn every important feature into a concrete life/work scenario: what the user can do, avoid, save, ship, or earn.
- Rank selling points by decision weight: most valuable first, secondary points later.
- Use progressive language to keep attention: "More importantly", "For growing teams", "What this unlocks", "Compared with".
- Make the recommended plan visibly easier to choose, but keep the other plans honest and useful.
- Explain price mechanics plainly when relevant: monthly/yearly, credits, seats, usage tiers, one-time payment, lifetime deal, or custom quote.

## SEO Requirements

- Use an H1 that includes the product/category and "Pricing" or "Price" when natural.
- Write a page-specific title and meta description aligned with visible pricing copy.
- Keep enough crawlable content around the cards: value proposition, plan summaries, comparison text, FAQ, and trust details.
- Add structured data when the implementation supports it:
  - `Product`/`SoftwareApplication`
  - `Offer` or plan-level offer data when prices are public
  - `FAQPage` for real pricing questions
- Cover buyer-intent questions in visible FAQ copy, such as:
  - Is there a free trial?
  - Can I cancel anytime?
  - What happens when I exceed limits?
  - Which plan should I choose?
  - Do you offer refunds, invoices, or enterprise billing?
- Avoid thin Pricing pages that contain only cards and buttons. They usually underperform for both SEO and conversion.

## Natural vs Paid Traffic Framing

For organic traffic, the Pricing page should educate and build trust before the CTA because visitors may still be comparing options. For paid traffic, keep the same value logic but make the offer, difference from alternatives, and CTA path more immediate.

## Review Checklist

- The page explains why the product is worth paying for before or alongside the price.
- There is at least one meaningful price anchor.
- The recommended plan has a clear buyer profile and reason to choose it.
- Key objections are answered without requiring a sales call.
- Plan limits and billing rules are understandable.
- FAQ copy is indexable, specific, and not generic filler.
- Schema and metadata reflect the actual product and current pricing.
